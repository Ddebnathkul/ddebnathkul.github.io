About

I am a PhD student in the EQUATION group of Prof. Jon Sundqvist at the Institute of Astronomy (KU Leuven). My work combines cutting‑edge 2D/3D radiation‑hydrodynamic simulations using MPI-AMRVAC in a unified approach, starting deep within the sub‑sonic regions and extending all the way upto the line‑driven wind region in hot and luminous, massive stars.

Our new unified atmosphere‑and‑wind models are the first to self‑consistently bridge the photosphere and supersonic outflow. They reveal that:

  • (1) Structure starts appearing deep within the sub‑sonic regions, originating from the iron‑opacity bump.
  • (2) The atmosphere becomes highly turbulent; spectra computed from our RHD models can reproduce the extra broadening observed in photospheric lines of O‑type stars, making turbulence a natural explanation for the ad‑hoc “macro‑turbulence” used in 1‑D synthesis codes.
  • (3) Turbulent pressure inflates the pressure‑scale height around the photosphere, altering inferred log g values and helping solve the long‑standing mass‑discrepancy problem.

I also co‑develop LIME — an on‑the‑fly mass‑loss estimator that couples modified CAK theory with line‑statistics parameters to predict mass‑loss rates for stars of arbitrary chemical composition.

When I’m not simulating stellar winds, I cultivate an ever-expanding collection of tropical plants—see the full greenhouse on the plants page.
